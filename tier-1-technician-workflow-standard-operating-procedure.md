<h1>Tier 1 Technician Workflow</h1>

<p><strong>Purpose:</strong> Establish a standardized workflow for Tier 1 technicians to triage, work, escalate, and resolve support tickets within the service desk environment.</p>

<h2>1. Start‑of‑Shift Routine</h2>
<p>Technicians begin each shift by reviewing the following queues in order:</p>
<ul>
  <li>High Priority (P1/P2)</li>
  <li>SLA Breached</li>
  <li>SLA At Risk</li>
  <li>Unassigned Tickets</li>
</ul>
<p>Work begins with the highest‑priority or SLA‑critical items.</p>

<h2>2. Priority Definitions</h2>
<p>Standard MSP priority levels:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>P1 – Critical:</strong> Major outage or business‑wide impact</li>
  <li><strong>P2 – High:</strong> Significant impact or key user blocked</li>
  <li><strong>P3 – Medium:</strong> Standard issues, routine impact</li>
  <li><strong>P4 – Low:</strong> Minor issues or general questions</li>
</ul>

<h2>3. Triage Workflow</h2>
<p>For each new ticket:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Review summary, description, reporter, priority, and SLA timers</li>
  <li>Send an acknowledgement</li>
  <li>Assign the ticket</li>
  <li>Perform Tier 1 troubleshooting</li>
  <li>Select the appropriate next action:</li>
  <ul>
    <li>Resolve</li>
    <li>In Progress</li>
    <li>Waiting for Customer</li>
    <li>Escalate to Tier 2</li>
  </ul>
</ul>

<h2>4. Tier 1 Troubleshooting Requirements</h2>
<p>Before escalation, Tier 1 must:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Verify the issue with the user</li>
  <li>Attempt basic troubleshooting</li>
  <li>Check for known issues or outages</li>
  <li>Document all steps taken</li>
  <li>Capture relevant error messages or screenshots</li>
</ul>

<p>Escalation is required when:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Administrative access is needed</li>
  <li>The issue exceeds Tier 1 scope</li>
  <li>Troubleshooting is exhausted</li>
  <li>SLA risk is high</li>
</ul>

<h2>5. Communication Standards</h2>
<p>All communication must be clear, concise, and professional.</p>

<p><strong>Acknowledgement</strong><br>
“Hi &lt;name&gt;, this request is being reviewed.”</p>

<p><strong>Waiting for Customer</strong><br>
“Additional information is required to continue troubleshooting: …”</p>

<p><strong>Resolution</strong><br>
“The issue appears resolved. Please test and confirm.”</p>

<p><strong>Internal Escalation Note</strong><br>
“Tier 1 troubleshooting completed: … Escalation required due to …”</p>

<h2>6. Escalation Workflow</h2>
<p>When escalating:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Add an internal note summarizing:</li>
  <ul>
    <li>Troubleshooting performed</li>
    <li>Findings</li>
    <li>Reason for escalation</li>
  </ul>
  <li>Update status to <strong>Escalated to Tier 2</strong></li>
  <li>Assign to the appropriate Tier 2 queue or group</li>
</ul>

<h2>7. Resolution and Closure</h2>
<p>Before resolving a ticket:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Confirm the issue is fixed</li>
  <li>Document final actions</li>
  <li>Notify the user</li>
  <li>Transition the ticket to <strong>Resolved</strong></li>
</ul>

<h2>8. Ticket Hygiene Standards</h2>
<p>Technicians must maintain:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Accurate statuses</li>
  <li>Timely updates</li>
  <li>Complete documentation</li>
  <li>Professional communication</li>
  <li>SLA compliance</li>
</ul>
